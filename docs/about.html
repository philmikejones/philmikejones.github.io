About me¶

I’m a lecturer at the University of Derby, where I teach social science research methods. I specialise in quantitative and spatial analysis. Previously I worked on the “Long-term impacts of ‘Thatcherism’: crime, politics and inequality” project.

I have a PhD in human geography. You can read the manuscript or view the source code.

I write R and Python code to analyse spatial and non–spatial data sets. You can see some of my code projects on GitHub. I’ve written several tutorials on R, Python, GIS, and Linux. My stats101 website might also be useful if you want an introduction to statistics (written with Python).

I write code to analyse spatial and non–spatial data sets. I am the author of rakeR, an R package for simulating spatial data. You can see some of my other code projects on GitHub.

Education¶

I have a PhD in human geography from the University of Sheffield (2018). My thesis is, ‘A Spatial Microsimulation Analysis of Health Inequalities and Health Resilience’. You can read the manuscript or view the source code. I was supervised by Professors Dimitris Ballas and Liddy Goyder, with support from Doncaster Metropolitan Borough Council, especially Laurie Mott.

I have an MA with distinction in social research from the University of York (2010), and a BA (Hons) in Sociology from the University of York (2005).

Current role¶

I am currently a lecturer in policing (research methods) at the University of Derby in the Department of Criminology and Social Sciences. I previously worked on the Long-term impacts of 'Thatcherism': crime, politics and inequality project.
